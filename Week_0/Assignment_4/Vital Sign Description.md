Vital Sign: Systolic Blood Pressure (SBP)

Systolic Blood Pressure (SBP) measures the pressure in the arteries when the heart contracts and pumps blood to the rest
of the body. In adults, a normal SBP is typically less than 120 mmHg. Values between 120 and 139 mmHg are considered
elevated and may indicate an increased risk of developing hypertension. A systolic pressure of 140 mmHg or higher is
classified as hypertension, which can increase the risk of serious health complications such as stroke, heart disease,
and kidney damage. On the other hand, an SBP below 90 mmHg is considered hypotension and may lead to symptoms such as
dizziness, fainting, or inadequate blood flow to vital organs. Monitoring SBP is important in clinical settings because
it provides key information about cardiovascular health and overall circulatory function.
