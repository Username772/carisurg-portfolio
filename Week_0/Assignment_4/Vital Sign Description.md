### **Vital Sign: Respiratory Rate (RR)** 

<p align="justify">
Respiratory rate (RR) is the number of breaths a person takes per minute and is an important 
indicator of respiratory and overall physiological function. In healthy adults, a normal respiratory 
rate is typically between 12 and 20 breaths per minute. A respiratory rate above 20 breaths per 
minute is known as tachypnoea and may indicate conditions such as respiratory infection, 
asthma, anxiety, fever, sepsis, or respiratory distress. Conversely, a respiratory rate below 12 
breaths per minute is called bradypnoea and may occur in cases of drug overdose, head injury, or 
neurological impairment. Severe abnormalities in respiratory rate can lead to inadequate oxygen 
delivery and may become life-threatening if not treated promptly. In triage settings, respiratory 
rate is especially important because it is often one of the earliest signs of patient deterioration. 
Triage nurses use respiratory rate measurements to assess the severity of illness, identify patients 
requiring urgent intervention, and monitor the effectiveness of treatment. Accurate monitoring of 
RR helps healthcare professionals recognise critical conditions early and improve patient 
outcomes.
</p>
